
World coffee exports rose by 9 per cent to 8.76 million bags of 60 kg each in June this year on the back of increasing global demand.  The global export of the brew stood at 8.02 million bags in the year-ago period, according to the International Coffee Organisation (ICO) data.
In the first nine months of the current coffee year (October-September), coffee shipments rose by 16 per cent to 80.7 million bags compared to 69.7 million bags in the same period of the previous year.
In the twelve months ending June 2011, exports of Arabica totalled 68.4 million bags compared to 59.6 million bags in the year-ago period. Robusta exports amounted to 36.5 million bags.
