
Gold prices on Wednesday breached the crucial Rs 26,000 mark for the first time ever tracking strong trends in global markets where financial uncertainty increased the metal’s appeal as a safe investment haven. With international prices rallying, the yellow metal added Rs 214 to Rs 26,055 per 10 grams in New Delhi thereby crossing the psychological level of 26,000 for the first time after continuing with its five-day long rising streak. Gold of 99.9 and 99.5 per cent purity advanced by Rs 215 each to Rs 26,055 and Rs 25,935 per 10 grams respectively, while sovereigns shed Rs 100 to 20,900 per piece of eight grams.
Immediately after the S&P’s cut the US credit rating from AAA to AA+, the gold prices surged by $44.40 to $1,707.80 an ounce in Singapore amid slump in equities and the dollar.
However, gold rose to an all-time high of $1,782.50 an ounce in New York, especially after the US Federal Reserve on Tuesday, seeking to bolster the economy pledged to keep its benchmark interest rate at a record low through at least mid-2013, which boosted demand for the metal as a haven investment.
Rising trend
Besides, a rising trend in the futures trade and buying by retailers to meet the marriage season demand further influenced the sentiment.
Bullion experts aver that the spurt in demand for gold is mainly due to US credit rating downgrade and a faltering economy, which boosted the demand for protection of wealth.
Maximum upsurge in prices was seen after Standard and Poor’s announcement of downgrading the US credit rating from AAA to AA+, which spurred a rout in global equities and stoked concern that the US may lapse into another recession, they added
